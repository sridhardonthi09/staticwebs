Sankhya Yoga

Transcendental Knowledge

In this pivotal chapter, Krishna begins His divine instruction by explaining the eternal nature of the soul and the temporary nature of the body. This fundamental teaching about the immortal soul forms the foundation for understanding dharma, duty, and the path to liberation from the cycle of birth and death.

‚öñÔ∏è Dharma üìø 72 Verses üëÅÔ∏è Soul Knowledge

In this foundational chapter on Sankhya Yoga, Krishna begins His divine instruction by addressing Arjuna's moral crisis. He explains the eternal nature of the soul, the temporary nature of the body, and the importance of performing one's duty without attachment. This chapter establishes the philosophical foundation for the entire Bhagavad Gita.

‚ú® The Eternal Soul

Krishna reveals the most fundamental truth: the soul is eternal, indestructible, and beyond birth and death. Just as a person changes clothes, the soul changes bodies. Understanding this truth removes the fear of death and the illusion of loss, enabling one to perform duty with clarity and determination.
