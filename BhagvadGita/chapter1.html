Arjuna Vishada Yoga

The Dejection of Arjuna

This chapter sets the stage for the entire Bhagavad Gita. As the two armies face each other on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna surveys the warriors and is overcome with grief at the prospect of fighting his relatives, teachers, and friends. His moral crisis leads him to lay down his bow and seek guidance from Krishna.

In this opening chapter on Arjuna Vishada Yoga, the stage is set for the entire Bhagavad Gita. As the two armies prepare for battle at Kurukshetra, Arjuna is overcome with grief and moral confusion seeing his relatives, teachers, and friends ready to fight against each other. His crisis of conscience sets up the need for Krishna's divine instruction.

‚ú® The Divine Setup

This chapter masterfully creates the dramatic context for spiritual instruction. Arjuna's breakdown is not weakness but the awakening of conscience that questions the value of material gain at the cost of destroying relationships and dharma. His surrender to Krishna as guru opens the door for receiving transcendental knowledge.
