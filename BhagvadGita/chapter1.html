Arjuna Vishada Yoga

The Dejection of Arjuna

This chapter sets the stage for the entire Bhagavad Gita. As the two armies face each other on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna surveys the warriors and is overcome with grief at the prospect of fighting his relatives, teachers, and friends. His moral crisis leads him to lay down his bow and seek guidance from Krishna.

üìñ Chapter Summary

The opening chapter establishes the epic battlefield where the Bhagavad Gita unfolds. King Dhritarashtra inquires about the events at Kurukshetra, and through Sanjaya's narration, we witness Duryodhana's assessment of both armies and the positioning of great warriors. This chapter sets the stage for Arjuna's moral crisis that will unfold in subsequent verses, highlighting the tension between duty, family bonds, and righteousness.

This chapter reminds us that life often presents us with complex situations where we must choose sides. Like Kurukshetra, our daily life is a battlefield between our higher and lower nature.
