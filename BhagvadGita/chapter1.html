Arjuna Vishada Yoga

The Dejection of Arjuna

This chapter sets the stage for the entire Bhagavad Gita. As the two armies face each other on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna surveys the warriors and is overcome with grief at the prospect of fighting his relatives, teachers, and friends. His moral crisis leads him to lay down his bow and seek guidance from Krishna.

üå∫ Chapter Summary

The first chapter of the Bhagavad Gita establishes the dramatic context for Krishna's teachings. Arjuna's emotional and moral crisis represents the human condition when faced with difficult choices. His compassion for all beings, though admirable, paralyzes him from performing his duty. This sets the stage for Krishna's profound teachings that will guide Arjuna (and all of us) toward right action and spiritual realization.

üîë Key Themes:

  • Dharma vs Personal Attachment: The conflict between duty and emotional bonds
  • Compassion and Action: How to act with love while fulfilling responsibilities
  • Spiritual Crisis: The beginning of deeper spiritual seeking
  • Guru-Disciple Relationship: Arjuna's surrender to Krishna as teacher
