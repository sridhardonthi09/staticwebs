Ksetra Ksetrajna Vibhaga Yoga

The Field and the Knower of the Field

In this profound chapter, Krishna explains the difference between the material body (the field) and the conscious soul (the knower of the field). He describes the constituents of material nature, the symptoms of knowledge, and the ultimate goal of distinguishing between matter and spirit to attain the Supreme.

In this profound chapter on Ksetra Ksetrajna Vibhaga Yoga, Krishna explains the fundamental distinction between matter and spirit - between the material body (ksetra, the field) and the conscious soul (ksetrajna, the knower of the field). This knowledge is essential for spiritual realization, as it enables one to distinguish between the temporary physical form and the eternal spiritual essence within.

üîç The Ultimate Discrimination

Understanding the difference between the body and the soul is the foundation of spiritual wisdom. Krishna explains that the body is merely a field of activities, while consciousness itself is the knower who experiences through this field. Most importantly, Krishna reveals that He is the supreme knower present in all fields, making this teaching not just philosophical but deeply devotional, connecting individual consciousness with universal consciousness.
