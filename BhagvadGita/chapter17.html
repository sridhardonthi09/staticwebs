Sraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga

The Divisions of Faith

In this enlightening chapter, Krishna explains the three types of faith that exist according to the three modes of nature. He describes how faith in goodness, passion, and ignorance manifests differently in worship, food preferences, charity, austerities, and sacrifices. This teaching helps devotees understand how to cultivate sattvic faith and practices that lead to spiritual advancement and divine realization.

üôè Chapter Summary

‚ú® Sattvic Faith üìø 28 Verses üïâÔ∏è Om Tat Sat

In this profound chapter on Sraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga, Krishna explains how faith manifests according to the three modes of material nature. He describes how sattvic, rajasic, and tamasic faith express themselves differently in worship, food choices, charity, austerities, and sacrifices. The chapter concludes with the sacred formula "Om Tat Sat," revealing how all spiritual activities should be performed with proper consciousness of the Supreme Absolute Truth.

üôè The Three Faces of Faith

Krishna teaches that faith is not uniform but varies according to one's acquired nature. Those in goodness worship divine beings and prefer pure foods, those in passion worship powerful entities and enjoy stimulating foods, while those in ignorance worship ghosts and spirits and consume stale food. The ultimate teaching is that all spiritual practices should be sanctified with "Om Tat Sat" to connect us with the Supreme Truth beyond the modes of nature.
