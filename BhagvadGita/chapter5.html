Karma Sanyasa Yoga

Path of Renunciation of Action

In this illuminating chapter, Krishna clarifies the apparent contradiction between action and renunciation. He explains that both Karma Yoga and Sanyasa lead to liberation, but reveals which path is superior and more practical. This chapter teaches the art of true renunciation - not the abandonment of action, but performing action without attachment to results.

‚öñÔ∏è Balance of Paths üìø 29 Verses üßò True Renunciation

In this clarifying chapter on Karma Sanyasa Yoga, Krishna resolves Arjuna's confusion about the apparent contradiction between action and renunciation. He reveals that both paths lead to liberation, but explains why Karma Yoga (action with devotion) is superior to mere renunciation of action. This chapter teaches the essence of true renunciation - freedom from attachment, not abandonment of duty.

‚ú® The Art of True Renunciation

Krishna explains that real renunciation is not the physical abandonment of activities, but the mental renunciation of attachment to results. A true renunciant is one who neither hates nor desires, remaining equipoised in all circumstances. This internal renunciation, combined with continued performance of duty, is the highest form of spiritual practice and leads to complete liberation.
