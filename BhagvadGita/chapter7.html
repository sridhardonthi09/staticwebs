Path of Knowledge and Realization

In this enlightening chapter, Krishna reveals His divine nature and the secret of knowing Him completely. He explains the difference between theoretical knowledge and realized knowledge, describes His various energies, and reveals why so few understand His true nature. This chapter provides the key to transcendent knowledge that leads to complete devotion and liberation.

In this profound chapter on Jnana Vijnana Yoga, Krishna reveals the ultimate knowledge about His divine nature and energies. He explains the difference between theoretical knowledge (jnana) and realized knowledge (vijnana), describes His material and spiritual energies, and reveals why so few people truly understand Him. This chapter provides the key to transcendent knowledge that leads to complete devotion and liberation.

‚ú® The Supreme Science of God

Krishna unveils the science of His divine nature, explaining that He has both inferior (material) and superior (spiritual) energies. The eight material elements form His inferior nature, while the living entities constitute His superior energy. Understanding this distinction and Krishna's role as the source of all energies is the key to complete knowledge that leaves nothing more to be known.
