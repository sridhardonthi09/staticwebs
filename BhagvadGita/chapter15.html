Purushottama Yoga

The Supreme Person

In this climactic chapter, Krishna reveals His supreme identity as Purushottama - the Supreme Person who transcends both the fallible and infallible beings. Using the metaphor of the cosmic tree, He explains the illusory nature of material existence and the path to attain His supreme abode. This chapter presents the ultimate goal of spiritual life: surrender to the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

üëë Supreme Person üìø 20 Verses üå≥ Cosmic Tree

This culminating chapter on Purushottama Yoga reveals Krishna's supreme identity as the ultimate person who transcends all categories of existence. Using the profound metaphor of the inverted cosmic tree, Krishna shows how material existence is an illusion that must be transcended. The path involves cutting the tree of attachment with the weapon of detachment and seeking refuge in the Supreme Person from whom all existence emanates.

üëë The Supreme Destination

Krishna reveals Himself as Purushottama - the Supreme Person who is beyond both the fallible material world and the infallible spiritual realm. This chapter provides the ultimate goal: complete surrender to the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Those who understand this truth and surrender with pure hearts attain His eternal abode, from which there is no return to material suffering.
