Purushottama Prapti Yoga

The Supreme Divine Personality

This chapter uses the metaphor of an inverted cosmic tree to describe material existence and explains the nature of the Supreme Person (Purushottama) who transcends both the perishable and imperishable aspects of creation. Krishna reveals Himself as the source of all existence and the ultimate goal of all knowledge.

üå≥ Chapter Summary

Chapter 15 presents the profound metaphor of an inverted cosmic tree to describe material existence, with roots in the spiritual realm and branches extending into the material world. Krishna reveals Himself as Purushottama - the Supreme Person who transcends both the perishable (material) and imperishable (individual soul) aspects of creation. The chapter emphasizes the need to cut attachment to this material tree with the weapon of detachment and surrender to the Supreme Lord, who is the source of all knowledge, light, and existence.

üîë Key Teachings:

  • Cosmic Tree Metaphor: Material existence as an inverted tree that must be transcended
  • Eternal Soul Parts: All living beings are eternal fragments of the Supreme
  • Source of Light: Krishna as the source of illumination in sun, moon, and fire
  • Inner Witness: The Supreme resides in everyone's heart as the source of memory and knowledge
  • Purushottama: Krishna as the Supreme Person beyond both perishable and imperishable
  • Ultimate Goal: All Vedic knowledge points to understanding Krishna as the Supreme
